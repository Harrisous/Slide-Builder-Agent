MECHANISM OF ACTION OVERVIEW (LATER‑LINE mCRC)

SELECTIVE VEGFR-1/2/3 INHIBITION DRIVES SUSTAINED ANTI‑ANGIOGENIC ACTIVITY

FRUZAQLA (fruquintinib) is an oral small‑molecule kinase inhibitor of VEGFR‑1, VEGFR‑2, and VEGFR‑3.1–3

It is described as a novel, selective inhibitor of all 3 VEGF receptors with limited off‑target kinase activity, enabling drug exposure that supports sustained target inhibition.1–3,13*

  • • Limits off‑target kinase activity, allowing for **high drug exposure** and **sustained target inhibition**.¹³
  • • Anti‑angiogenic mechanism: **restricts tumor growth and progression (VEGFR‑1/‑2)**.¹³,¹⁶
  • • Potential to inhibit **lymphangiogenesis (VEGFR‑3)**.¹³,¹⁷

Kinome/VEGFR selectivity visualization (source figure).

By focusing inhibition on VEGFR‑1/2/3 with limited off‑target activity, FRUZAQLA is positioned to support an anti‑angiogenic approach that aligns with later‑line mCRC goals: durable disease control with tolerability and a clinically manageable safety profile consistent with selective targeting as described in the cited materials.1–3,13

TRIAL DESIGN & METHODS (FRESCO)

FRESCO CONFIRMS OS AS THE PRIMARY ENDPOINT IN A RANDOMIZED, DOUBLE-BLIND PHASE 3 mCRC TRIAL

Phase 3, multicenter, randomized (2:1), double-blind, placebo-controlled trial conducted in a single country (China).1,2

Compact CONSORT-style study schematic (ITT N=416; 2:1 randomization; arms; endpoints).

Population (key eligibility): adults 18–75 years with mCRC and ECOG PS 0–1; progression during/after prior fluoropyrimidine-, oxaliplatin-, or irinotecan-based chemotherapy; no prior VEGFR inhibitor therapy.1,2

  • • Stratification factors: prior VEGF inhibitor therapy (yes vs no)2
  • • Stratification factors: K-RAS mutational status (wild type vs mutant)2

Intervention: fruquintinib 5 mg PO QD + BSC on a 3-weeks-on/1-week-off schedule (28-day cycle). Treatment continued until progression, death, unacceptable toxicity, or withdrawal.1,2

Control: placebo + BSC on the same 3-weeks-on/1-week-off schedule (28-day cycle).1,2

Endpoints hierarchy: Primary endpoint: Overall survival (OS).1,2 Key secondary endpoints included PFS, objective response rate, disease control rate, duration of response, and safety.1,2

Assessments: Tumor assessments and safety monitoring were performed per protocol throughout treatment until discontinuation; survival follow-up continued for OS.2

*The FRESCO study was conducted in China.1,2

SAFETY / TOLERABILITY (FRESCO)

PREDICTABLE, MANAGEABLE SAFETY PROFILE IN FRESCO

In FRESCO, the majority of adverse reactions (ARs) with FRUZAQLA were described as predictable (consistent with VEGF/VEGFR pathway inhibition) and manageable.1–3 Despite predictability, individual patient experiences may vary.

Serious ARs occurred in 15% of patients treated with FRUZAQLA + BSC; serious ARs in ≥2% included intestinal obstruction (2.9%) and hemorrhage (2.2%).1 Fatal ARs occurred in 2.5% (7 patients).1

Adverse reactions occurring in ≥10% of patients (FRESCO).1

  • • Hypertension: 61% all grades; 23% grade 3/4 (vs 17% all grades; 2.2% grade 3/4 with placebo + BSC).1
  • • PPES/hand–foot skin reaction: 49% all grades; 11% grade 3/4 (vs 2.9%; 0%).1
  • • Proteinuria: 55% all grades; 4.7% grade 3/4 (vs 30%; 0%).1
  • • Diarrhea: 25% all grades; 3.6% grade 3/4 (vs 5%; 0%).1
  • • Fatigue: 25% all grades; 2.5% grade 3/4 (vs 13%; 1.5%).1

Dose interruptions/reductions and discontinuations due to ARs (FRESCO).1,2

Clinical management principles (monitoring + early intervention)

  • Baseline and on-treatment monitoring: check blood pressure regularly; monitor urine protein per PI; assess for PPES/skin changes, diarrhea, fatigue, bleeding, and symptoms of GI obstruction/perforation risk.1
  • Prompt supportive care: initiate/optimize antihypertensives; early skin care and activity modifications for PPES; antidiarrheals and hydration for diarrhea; evaluate and manage fatigue contributors.
  • Dose modification when needed: use dose interruptions and/or reductions to manage tolerability; discontinue for severe or life-threatening toxicity per PI.1
  • Escalate for serious events: urgently evaluate suspected hemorrhage, thromboembolic/ischemic events, severe infection, or GI obstruction; manage per standard of care and PI guidance.1

DOSING & DOSE MODIFICATION (SELECT ARs) | LATER-LINE mCRC

OPTIMIZE FRUZAQLA TOLERABILITY WITH PROACTIVE DOSE HOLDS & STEP-DOWN RESUMPTION

Administration & monitoring focus: use structured toxicity checks to support sustained therapy in later-line mCRC.

Practical monitoring checklist (clinic + home):
BP: baseline and regularly during treatment; encourage home BP log.
Urine protein: baseline and periodically; escalate to 24-hour urine when indicated.
Skin: routine hand/foot skin checks for early HFSR; reinforce emollients and friction avoidance.
GI symptoms: assess stool frequency/volume and hydration status; intervene early for diarrhea.

Brief patient counseling points: report new/worsening fatigue, abdominal pain, dark urine/jaundice, foamy urine/edema, persistent diarrhea, or painful hand/foot skin changes promptly; early reporting enables dose holds/reductions and may help maintain time on therapy.

  • • Dose modification approach: withhold for clinically significant toxicity, then resume at the next lower dose level when resolved per guidance.
  • • Key AR schema to operationalize in practice: hypertension, HFSR, proteinuria, diarrhea (monitor early; intervene promptly; consider temporary interruption and dose reduction as needed).
  • • Escalate evaluation for signs of hepatic injury (LFTs, bilirubin) and renal injury (proteinuria workup) per thresholds in prescribing guidance.
